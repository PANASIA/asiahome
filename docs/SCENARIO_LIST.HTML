Scenarios

A scenario is a predefined set of milestones that support a project manager in initiating and planning a project initiated by the STACIS(R) Chamber program.

 

s1 - Select a UDS Solution

Even though STACIS(R) Chamber methodology focuses primarily on implementation, selection is an essential part, because the outputs of the selection (workshop report, project plan, contract, etc.) are the starting point of the implementation.

The Implementation Scenarios

Implementation projects are performed within the phases Visioning, Proof of Concept and Implementation. On the basis of UDS’s prototyping principles, each project consists of the same activities within the same sequence. Only the context in which those activities are performed differs.

For example: simulation 1 during Visioning has the same activities as simulation 1 during Implementation. Within Visioning, the customer aims to build an enterprise wide business model and within Implementation the objective is to build a site-specific business model. That is why UDS developed one implementation milestone plan which is used to configure four implementation scenarios. This results in a situation where depending on the scenario, some milestones need not to be executed or need to be combined. The four implementation scenarios are:

  • i1 - Prepare a Corporate Kernel Model
  • i2 - Implement based on Standard Model
  • i3 - Implement based on Customized Model
  • i4 - Prepare Model and Implement

i1 - Prepare a Corporate Kernel Model

Scenario i1 is a set of milestones for building a Corporate Kernel Model. A Corporate Kernel Model is a business of the customer organization on corporate or global level. This scenario is used during the Visioning phase and ends with simulation 2, an accepted business model. Migration is done during Proof of Concept or Implementation.

i1 - Milestone Plan to prepare a Corporate Kernel Model.

The scenario starts with the definition of a project plan and project organization. After installation of (temporary) hardware and software, initiation of system/application/model management, and preparation of the company, the training program starts. Key users are trained on the business control models and principles to enable them to define their own business concept and control model. Once the business concepts and models are defined, the key users are trained on UDS Solution software functionality, the EAM tool, and EAM modeling. Immediately after training, work groups configure business processes including input and output. This process ends with a simulation conducted by the key users and UDS consultants showing the business control model, business functions and processes, and the UDS software configuration.

The pilot stage starts with an inventory and description of the organizational impact caused by the designed business model. After approval of the organizational changes, the targeted situation is finalized by creating a conversion plan, generating procedures and work instructions, coding master data, and finalizing system/application management. After finishing these milestones, the final business model is defined and shown by key users in simulation 2.

 

i2 - Implement based on Standard Model

This scenario focus on a rapid implementation by system improvement rather than organizational changes. This scenario is used:

  • during the roll-out after Visioning and Proof of Concept have resulted in a customer-specific business model (reference or kernel model);
  • when a customer, most of the time operating within the small and medium enterprise market, wants to do a rapid implementation based on UDS’s Microsoft BackOffice Certified products.

Scenario i2 concerns milestones for an implementation that is based on an enterprise reference model which represents the way of doing business in the context of a certain organization typology.

The milestone bubbles that are connected within a gray box, must be combined to compress the implementation approach. The milestone titles for the combined bubbles are shown within a framework.

  • This scenario makes use of only the Base Module. Model Building is out of the scope, due to the fact that this scenario assumes the use of an Enterprise Reference Model with a good fit. This scenario also assumes that no Customizations and Interfaces are needed.

i2 - Milestone Plan to implement based on a Standard Model.

The implementation phase starts with the delivery of a project plan and project setup. After hardware and software installation, key users are trained on the use of the UDS Solution processes and functionality which are based on an Enterprise Reference Model. During the training, an issue list is drawn up. This concurrent training and validation of the Enterprise Reference Model represents Simulation 1. Adaptations and/or enhancements on the Reference Model are tested during Simulation 2 which closes the mixed Mapping/Piloting stage. The deliverable is a customer specific UDS software configuration with organizational procedures.

In the migration stage, system, personnel and organization are prepared to transfer to the new situation. The information system is configured and master data is entered and/or converted. End user training and the final acceptance test with real data is done at the same time which is known as Simulation 3. Finally, the system is transferred to its owner, the customer organization, and goes live.

 

i3 - Implement based on Customized Model

Scenario i3 describes a set of milestones that does not include any model building activities. This scenario only uses the Base Module.

i3 - Milestone Plan to implement based on a Customized Model

The implementation phase starts with the definition of a project plan and project organization. After installation of hardware and software, initiation of system and application management (not model management), the company is informed about what is going to happen, the involvement and consequences. The business control model and scope need to be accepted which forms the basis for the key user training on the application and simulation 1.

Starting point for the piloting phase is that the customer approves the organizational changes implied by the business model of simulation 1. During the piloting stage, the targeted situation is finalized by creating a conversion plan, generating procedures and work instructions, coding master data, finalizing system/application management. After finishing these milestones, the final business model is defined and shown by key users in simulation 2.

In the migration stage, system, personnel and organization are prepared to transfer to the new situation. The information system is configured and master data is entered and/or converted. The end user training is performed by the owners of the solution: the key users. Key users make use of the business process editor within the Enterprise Modeler to train their end users. After training, the final acceptance test, or simulation 3, with real company data can be carried out by end users. Finally the system is transferred to its owner, the customer organization, and goes live.

 

i4 - Prepare Model and Implement

Scenario i4 describes a single site implementation including the building, piloting and implementation of a business model. The scenario makes use of the Base Module as well as the add-on modules Model Building and Customizations & Interfaces, which are of course optional.

i4 - Milestone Plan to prepare and Implement Model

The implementation phase starts with the definition of a project plan and project organization. After installation of (temporary) hardware and software, initiation of system, application and model management and preparation of the company, the training program starts. Key users are trained on the business control models and principles to enable them to define their own business concept and control model. Once the business concepts and models are defined, the key users are trained on UDS Solution software functionality, the EAM tool and modeling. Immediately after training, work groups configure business processes with their input and output. This process ends with a simulation conducted by the key users and UDS consultants showing the business control model, business functions and processes and the UDS software configuration.

The pilot stage starts with an inventory and a description of the organizational impact caused by the designed business model. After approval of the organizational changes, the targeted situation is finalized by creating a conversion plan, generating procedures and work instructions, coding master data, and finalizing system/application management. After finishing these milestones, the final business model is defined and shown by key users in simulation 2.

In the migration stage, system, personnel and organization are prepared to transfer to the new situation. The information system is configured and master data is entered and/or converted. The end user training is performed by the owners of the solution (key users). Key users utilize the business process editor within the Enterprise Modeler to train their end users. After training, the final acceptance test or simulation 3 with real company data is carried out by end users. Finally the system is transferred to its owner, the customer organization, and goes live.

 

c1 - Define and Implement Competence Center

The Competence Center scenario is a milestone approach for setting up a project for implementation of a Competence Center. The Competence Center scenario is part of UDS’s Competence Program.

The phases within the Competence Center scenario are not connected to the phases of STACIS(R) Chamber. The implementation of a Competence Center can be initiated at different moments during the implementation program depending of the situation and demands of the customer.

Below the scenario for setting up a Competence Center (CC) is shown.

c1 - The Competence Center Scenario

The objective of the Define To-Be is to define the functions of a Competence Center (CC) and to draw up a preliminary competence organization. The starting point for the Define To-Be definition is the life cycle of the implementation and operation-driven IT functions. The life cycle influences the Competence Mission, which is the strategic statement that serves as the basis for the CC. Based on the Competence Mission, the customer selects support functions for their specific situation from the Function Reference Model embedded within the Enterprise Modeler. Besides the selection of functions, a definition is given to the organizational entity that carries out the function in relation to the central/decentralized issues. Examples of organizational entities are the IT department, the line organization (central or decentralized) and of course the Competence Center despite the question of whether the Competence Center is part of the IT department. Finally, a preliminary organization chart can be drawn up. The CC is defined ‘To-Be.’

The Assess As Is objective is to analyze if the customer is ready to implement a Competence Center. The result is a ‘Get Ready’ plan which fills the gaps assessed during the Readiness Assessment. The ‘Get Ready’ plan, which consists, for example of functional training, change management workshops, and so on, prepares the customer for a common Competence Center implementation. Within the Assessment, the focus of this survey is on the To Be functions. For each functional area, Work Groups are selected and a detailed assessment is performed through the use of workshops. The results of those workshops are displayed in Spider Charts. After the Spider Charts have been analyzed and findings reported, the ‘Get Ready’-plan is drawn up.

The objective of the CC Implementation Planning is to define a final Competence Organization supplemented with a Competence Center Implementation Plan. Making use of the preliminary defined organization, the Competence Center organization is finalized and supplemented with role profiles. In addition, the CC Implementation Plan, divided into initiation, configuration and implementation, is drawn up. The ‘Get Ready’ plan with the gap-closing strategies concerning resources, processes, infrastructure and measurement, is used as input.

The objective of the CC initiation is to begin with the set-up of a Competence Center. This is done by allocating and/or recruiting CC staff and training core CC members. Training refers to classroom training or training ‘on the job’ which is performed by CC members who are participating within the Visioning or Proof of Concept phase.

The objective of the CC configuration is to prepare the IT organization for the implementation of the new IT functions. Implementation of functions can be related to the line organization (central and decentralized), IT organization and/or Competence Center. Preparations must be made for:

  • the definition of Service Levels for internal customers
  • the evaluation and selection of external suppliers (supplier contracts)
  • the definition of processes
  • the evaluation and selection of infrastructure tools

The objective of the CC Implementation is to get the Competence Center operational. The Competence Center staff must be recruited/allocated and trained. Special attention is paid to the training of Competence Center staff realized by ‘How-to training’ or ‘On the job training’. The classroom training sessions and the participation in the implementation project (e.g. Visioning, Proof of Concept) are tools for rapidly acquisition of knowledge. Apart from looking at resources, Supplier Contracts are also closed and Services Levels are agreed upon. After implementation of the processes and (support) tools, the Competence Center starts to deliver its services to its (internal) customer.
